Introduction To Your Cathar Experience

A Brief History Of The Cathar Country & Castles

Up until forty years ago, nobody had heard of the "Cathar country". It was first used by the local tourist service of the Aude departement of southern France, to create a coherence between many of the remarkable local historic monumnets, that had witnessed sieges and massacres that marked the areas turbulent history in and around the twelfth and thirteenth centuries.

The "Cathars" were not a race, or a people; they were followers of a dissident church that flourished in several parts of Europe during the early Medieval period. Catharism promoted values of equality, neighbourliness and charity and turned it's back on the pomp, hierarchy and worldly wealth of the Catholic church of the time.

By the early 13th century, Catharism had taken such a strong hold in this area of France, that in 1208 Pope Innocent III launched the notorious Albigensian Crusade - a crusade aimed at the "heretical" Cathars.

The fortified hilltops, castles, villages and towns you will see on your tour in the "Cathar country" still remain to this day with extraordinary panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. They serve as a stark reminder of the turbulent history of the area. during the Albigensian crusade period, many castles and other fortified positions served as strongholds for the beseiged Cathars, where many witnessed atrocious massacres.
